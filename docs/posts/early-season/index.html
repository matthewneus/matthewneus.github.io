Maryland men’s basketball began the season as well as coach Kevin Willard could have asked for. His star freshman recored a 22 point, 20 rebound game, and the Terps won by 30.

Maryland’s defense suffocated Manahattan, allowing it to record a 27 percent shooting percent. But the Terps’ offense didn’t shoot lights out in the 79-point display. They only shot 44 percent from the field.

It’s only one game, but last season’s shooting struggles might not have completely left this year’s group. But looking at previous Maryland sides, a good defense might do enough to ensure a season the ends in the NCAA tournament.

Code 
library(tidyverse)
library(lubridate)
logs <- read_csv("https://raw.githubusercontent.com/dwillis/dwillis.github.io/main/docs/sports-data-files/cbblogs1525.csv")
logs <- logs |>
  mutate(month = month(Date), year = year(Date)) 

earlysznlogs <- logs |>
  filter(month == 11)

mdearlysznlogs <- logs |>
  group_by(Season) |>
  filter(Team == "Maryland" & month == 11) |>
  mutate(AvgFGPCT = mean(TeamFGPCT), AvgOPPFGPCT = mean(OpponentFGPCT))

md24 <- mdearlysznlogs |>
  group_by(year, AvgFGPCT, AvgOPPFGPCT) |>
  filter(Season == "2024-2025") |>
  mutate(TotalGames = sum(Game), year = year(Date))


md <- mdearlysznlogs |>
  group_by(year, AvgFGPCT, AvgOPPFGPCT) |>
  filter(Game == "1") |>
  filter(Season == "2015-2016" | Season == "2016-2017" | Season == "2017-2018" | Season == "2018-2019" | Season == "2019-2020" | Season == "2020-2021" | Season == "2021-2022" | Season == "2022-2023" | Season == "2023-2024") |>
  mutate(year = year(Date))

In early season games, which are just games played in November, Maryland averaged a shooting percentage of 47 percent and held their opponents to a 39 percent shooting percentage.

Code 
mdearlysznlogs |> 
  ungroup() |> 
  summarise(
    offense = mean(AvgFGPCT), 
    defense = mean(AvgOPPFGPCT)
    )

Maryland’s defensive effectiveness will be crucial when predicting if the Terps can return to the NCAA tournament. In the 2019-2020 season, Maryland’s opponents field goal percentage in November to 37 percent. In 2016-17, it was slighly higher at 39 percent. The Terps made the tourney in the 2016-2017 season, and was a lock for March Madness in 2019-2020. Even with lacking offensive shooting, Maryland found a way to succeed with great defense.

That carries over into years when Maryland begins well on both ends of the floor. The Terps made the tournament in 2022-23 with a 50 percent field goal percentage in November while keeping their opposition 38 percent. Maryland has made the NCAA tournament all but twice when keeping their opponents under 39 percent from the field, but it would have made it easily in a postponed 2019-20 campaign.

Maryland’s strong defensive start in 2024-25 can have fans believing of a NCAA tournament birth this season. The Terps’ defense has led them to the national tournament in years past despite a lackluster offensive shooting percentage.

The 2024-25 team could be a repeat of good defense leading to success. Although it’s only one game, a strong defensive performance limiting Manhattan in field goal percentage can predict another good defensive team this year. Previous Maryland teams have found success in preventing opponents from scoring, and this year’s team can do the same.

Maryland’s offense wasn’t great in its season opener against Manhattan. It only scored 79 points and shot 44 percent from the field. But its defense was effienct, only allowing 49 points. Maryland may not shoot teams out of the gym, but it may smother teams into low scoring outputs. Based on past Maryland teams, that will lead to a NCAA tournament birth.
